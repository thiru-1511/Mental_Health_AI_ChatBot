Mental Health Support Guide

1. Understanding Stress
Stress is a natural feeling of not being able to cope with specific demands and events. However, stress can become a chronic condition if a person does not take steps to manage it.
Symptoms: Headaches, sleep problems, fatigue, irritability, difficulty concentrating.
Coping Strategies:
- Deep Breathing: Take slow, deep breaths to trigger the body's relaxation response.
- Exercise: Physical activity produces endorphins that act as natural painkillers and mood elevators.
- Mindfulness: Focus on the present moment without judgment.

2. Managing Anxiety
Anxiety is your body's natural response to stress. It's a feeling of fear or apprehension about what's to come.
Tips for Anxiety:
- Rule of 3-3-3: Look around and name 3 things you see, 3 sounds you hear, and move 3 parts of your body.
- Limit Caffeine: Caffeine can trigger panic attacks in some people.
- Talk to Someone: Sharing your feelings with a trusted friend or therapist can help reduce the burden.

3. Depression Awareness
Depression is a mood disorder that causes a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest.
Signs: Persistent sad, anxious, or "empty" mood; feelings of hopelessness; loss of interest in hobbies.
Support:
- Professional Help is vital. Therapy and medication are effective treatments.
- Routine: Maintaining a daily schedule can help get back on track.
- Small Goals: Set small, manageable goals to feel a sense of accomplishment.

4. Self-Care Practices
- Sleep Hygiene: specific habits that can improve sleep quality.
- Nutrition: Eating a balanced diet.
- Social Connection: engaging with supportive family and friends.
